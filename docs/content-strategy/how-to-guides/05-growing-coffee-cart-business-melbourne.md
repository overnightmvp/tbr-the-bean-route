## Stage 3: Scaling Operations (Year 3-5)

You're booking 8-12 events/month and turning away work due to calendar conflicts. It's time to scale operations beyond solo work.

### Hiring Your First Barista

**When to Hire:**
- Turning away 3+ bookings/month due to scheduling conflicts
- Events over 100 guests requiring 2 baristas (currently declining or underperforming)
- Burnout risk (working 60+ hour weeks)

**Hiring Options:**

**Option 1: Casual Employee**
- **Pros:** Reliable, trained on your standards, available regularly
- **Cons:** Payroll complexity (tax, super, WorkCover insurance), commitment
- **Cost:** $30-40/hr (wage + super + insurance) = charge clients $60-80/hr (2Ã— labor cost)
- **Best For:** Regular bookings (2-3/week minimum) needing consistent help

**Option 2: Contractor/Freelance Barista**
- **Pros:** No payroll admin, pay per gig, flexible
- **Cons:** Less control over quality, availability not guaranteed, may work for competitors
- **Cost:** $40-50/hr (contractor rate) = charge clients $60-80/hr
- **Best For:** Occasional overflow events, backup for emergencies

**Option 3: Business Partner**
- **Pros:** Shared workload, shared investment, complementary skills
- **Cons:** Profit sharing, decision-making conflicts, legal complexity
- **Best For:** Scaling to multi-cart operation (partner manages second cart)

**Hiring Process:**
- Post on barista job boards (BaristaGuild, Gumtree, SEEK)
- Require 1-2 years commercial barista experience (hospitality background)
- Trial at 1-2 events (paid) before committing
- Train on your standards (bean handling, customer service, cart setup/breakdown)

**Example:** Hire casual barista at $35/hr, charge clients $70/hr for second barista (2Ã— markup = $35/hr profit on their labor)

## Stage 4: Business Maturity (Year 5+)

You're operating multiple carts, employing 2-5 baristas, and generating $80,000-150,000 annual revenue. Focus shifts to systematization and sustainable growth.

**Goal:** 200-300 events/year, $100,000-200,000 revenue, semi-passive operation

### Systematizing Operations

**System 1: Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)**

**Why It Matters:** Without documented processes, quality varies and training new staff takes weeks.

**Key SOPs to Document:**
- Event setup checklist (equipment, supplies, timeline)
- Barista service standards (greeting, coffee preparation, customer interaction)
- Breakdown and cleanup process
- Inventory management (bean ordering, supply restocking)
- Client communication templates (inquiry response, quote, booking confirmation)

**Format:** Simple Google Docs or PDF guides with photos/checklists

**Benefit:** New barista can learn systems in days, not weeks (scalability)

**System 2: Inventory and Supply Management**

**Problem:** Running out of beans mid-event or over-ordering supplies that expire

**Solution:**
- Track consumption per event type (corporate = 80 cups avg, wedding = 100 cups)
- Par stock system (always maintain 10kg beans, 500 cups, 20L milk on hand)
- Automate reordering (set calendar reminders or auto-ship from suppliers)

**System 3: Financial Tracking and Forecasting**

**Why It Matters:** You can't optimize what you don't measure.

**Metrics to Track Monthly:**
- Total events booked
- Total revenue (hourly vs per-cup bookings)
- Cost per event (beans, milk, supplies, labor, fuel)
- Profit margin per event type (weddings vs corporate vs markets)
- Client acquisition source (Instagram, The Bean Route, referrals, ads)

**Tools:**
- Simple spreadsheet (Google Sheets template)
- Accounting software (Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks for tax compliance)
- Track which marketing channels generate most bookings (double down on winners)

**Insight Example:** Discover weddings average 35% profit margin while corporate events average 25%â€”shift marketing to prioritize weddings.

## Common Growth Pitfalls to Avoid

Learn from other vendors' mistakes and scale sustainably:

**Pitfall 1: Scaling Too Fast**

**What Happens:** Buy second cart + hire 2 baristas in month 6, but only have 5 bookings/month
**Result:** Can't cover payroll and equipment financing, negative cash flow, business fails
**Fix:** Scale incrementallyâ€”hire first employee only when turning away 3+ bookings/month consistently for 3 months

**Pitfall 2: Neglecting Cash Flow**

**What Happens:** Invest $15,000 in premium machine but have only $2,000 cash reserves, then have slow month
**Result:** Can't pay suppliers or fuel costs, miss events, damage reputation
**Fix:** Maintain 3-6 months operating expenses in cash reserves before major investments

**Pitfall 3: Poor Hiring**

**What Happens:** Hire inexperienced barista to save money ($25/hr vs $35/hr), quality drops, clients complain
**Result:** Bad reviews, lost repeat bookings, damage to brand reputation
**Fix:** Hire experienced baristas (1-2 years minimum), pay market rate, invest in training

**Pitfall 4: Ignoring Marketing in Busy Seasons**

**What Happens:** Busy Oct-Dec, fully booked, stop all marketing. Jan-Mar = zero bookings (forgot to market during busy period)
**Result:** Revenue rollercoaster, inconsistent income
**Fix:** Market year-round (even when busy)â€”fill pipeline for slow months

**Pitfall 5: Underpricing to Compete**

**What Happens:** Competitor charges $120/hr, you drop to $110/hr to compete, they drop to $100, you drop to $90
**Result:** Race to bottom, both vendors unprofitable, unsustainable
**Fix:** Differentiate on quality (specialty beans, latte art, verified reviews), not price. Charge fair rates and attract ideal clients.
