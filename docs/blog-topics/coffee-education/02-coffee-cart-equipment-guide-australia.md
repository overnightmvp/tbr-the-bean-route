### The Coffee Cart Itself (600 words)

**Cart Types**

**Custom-Built Professional Carts**
- Features: Stainless steel, custom size, built-in water tanks, professional finish
- Cost: $10,000-25,000
- Best for: Serious business launch, financing available
- Australian suppliers: EVCartCo, Unicut, BeanTo, Custom Coffee Vans

**Trailer-Based Carts**
- Features: Towable trailer, larger setup, semi-permanent
- Cost: $15,000-40,000
- Best for: Festival circuit, market stalls, high-volume
- Requires: Vehicle with tow capacity

**DIY/Modified Carts**
- Features: Build yourself or modify existing cart
- Cost: $2,000-8,000 (materials + time)
- Best for: Handy individuals, tight budget
- Risk: Food safety compliance, professional finish

**Used Carts**
- Cost: 40-60% of new price ($4,000-12,000)
- Check: Structural integrity, water tanks, previous use
- Where: Gumtree, Facebook Marketplace, coffee equipment dealers

**Essential Cart Features**

*Water System*
- Fresh water tank: 90L minimum (serves ~180 cups)
- Wastewater tank: 36L minimum
- Pump system: Electric pump for machine pressure
- Filtration: Protect espresso machine ($200-500)

*Storage*
- Cup storage: Enclosed to protect from dust
- Bean storage: Cool, dark area (preserve freshness)
- Milk fridge space: Undercounter or side-mounted
- Supply storage: Lids, napkins, syrups, etc.

*Mobility*
- Locking wheels: Industrial grade (200kg+ capacity)
- Handle: Comfortable height for pushing
- Compact design: Fits through 90cm doorways

*Power Management*
- Wiring: Professional 15-amp setup
- Outlets: Multiple for machine, grinder, fridge
- Generator compatibility: If outdoor events

*Professional Touches*
- Menu board: Chalkboard or printed
- Branding space: Logo placement
- Weather protection: Roof, optional side panels
- Lighting: LED strip for evening events

**Australian Suppliers**
- EVCartCo (Melbourne): $12,000-22,000
- Unicut (Sydney): $10,000-18,000
- Custom Coffee Vans: Full custom builds
- BeanTo (Melbourne): Premium carts $15,000-25,000

### Espresso Machines (700 words)

**Machine Categories**

**Entry-Level Professional (1-Group)**
- Brands: Rancilio Silvia Pro, Breville Dual Boiler (prosumer)
- Cost: $2,000-4,000
- Capacity: 30-40 cups/hour max
- Best for: Starting out, testing market
- Limitations: Not truly commercial-grade (may struggle)

**Mid-Range Professional (1-Group)**
- Brands: Rancilio Classe 5, Nuova Simonelli Oscar II
- Cost: $5,000-8,000
- Capacity: 60-80 cups/hour
- Best for: Most mobile coffee cart businesses
- Features: PID temp control, reliable, repairable

**Premium Professional (2-Group)**
- Brands: La Marzocco Linea, Synesso, Slayer
- Cost: $12,000-25,000+
- Capacity: 100-150 cups/hour (dual baristas)
- Best for: High-volume events, premium branding
- Features: Exceptional temp stability, aesthetic appeal

**Key Features to Look For**

*Temperature Control*
- PID controller: ±0.5°C accuracy (essential)
- Dual boiler: Separate brew + steam (better consistency)
- Pre-infusion: Improves espresso quality (nice-to-have)

*Steam Power*
- Commercial steam wand: Fast milk texturing
- Steam pressure: 1.0-1.2 bar ideal
- Dual wands: If 2-group machine (dual baristas)

*Water Connection*
- Tank-fed capability: Essential for mobile (no plumbing)
- Pump pressure: Adjustable (9 bar standard)
- Water capacity: 3-5L tank

*Portability Considerations*
- Weight: 30-60kg (can you lift it into cart?)
- Size: Fits cart space (measure first!)
- Vibration resistance: Mobile cart = bumpy transport
- Power: 10-15 amp (Australian standard outlet compatible)

**New vs Used Machines**

*New Machine Benefits*
- Warranty: 1-2 years parts + labor
- Latest features: Updated PID, better steam
- Condition: Pristine, no wear
- Financing: Available from suppliers

*Used Machine Considerations*
- Cost: Save $2,000-8,000 (40-60% discount)
- Service history: Request maintenance records
- Inspection: Have technician check before buying
- Parts availability: Ensure model still supported
- Where to buy: Cafés closing, equipment dealers, auctions

**Australian Suppliers**
- Crema Coffee Garage (Melbourne): New + used, great service
- Five Senses Coffee (Multi-city): Equipment + training
- Veneziano Coffee Roasters: Machines + beans bundle
- Coffee Machines Australia: Online retailer

### Coffee Grinders (500 words)

**Why Grinder Matters**
- Espresso quality: 60% grinder, 40% machine (expert consensus)
- Freshness: Grind-on-demand (never pre-ground)
- Consistency: Even extraction = better taste

**Grinder Types**

**Stepped Grinders** (Entry-Level)
- Brands: Breville Smart Grinder Pro
- Cost: $300-600
- Pros: Affordable, repeatable settings
- Cons: Coarse adjustment steps (limits fine-tuning)
- Best for: Starting out, budget builds

**Stepless Grinders** (Professional)
- Brands: Mazzer Super Jolly, Eureka Mignon Specialita
- Cost: $800-1,500
- Pros: Infinite adjustment, consistent, reliable
- Cons: More expensive
- Best for: Serious coffee cart businesses

**On-Demand vs Doser**
- On-demand: Grinds per shot (fresher, less waste)
- Doser: Grinds into chamber (faster but grounds sit)
- Mobile cart: On-demand preferred (cleanliness)

**Key Features**

*Grind Quality*
- Burr type: Flat burrs (espresso) vs conical (versatility)
- Burr size: 64mm+ for commercial
- Grind consistency: Even particle size

*Speed*
- 5-7 seconds per double shot (on-demand)
- Important for queue management

*Durability*
- Commercial-rated: 20+ kg/week capacity
- Metal housing: Survives mobile transport

*Power & Size*
- Power: 300-500W (standard 10-amp outlet)
- Footprint: Fits on cart (check dimensions)

**Top Grinder Recommendations (Australia)**
- Mazzer Super Jolly ($1,200-1,600): Industry workhorse
- Eureka Mignon Specialita ($800-1,000): Compact, quiet, excellent
- Baratza Sette 270 ($600-800): Budget-friendly, good performance
- Mahlkönig E65S ($1,800-2,500): Premium, fast, commercial

**Grinder Maintenance**
- Daily: Purge first dose (stale grounds), wipe down
- Weekly: Deep clean burrs, vacuum chamber
- Monthly: Replace burrs every 500-800kg beans

### Milk Fridge & Storage (300 words)

**Undercounter Fridges**
- Size: 100-150L capacity (fits under cart)
- Temperature: 2-4°C (food safety compliance)
- Brands: Austune, Williams, Bromic
- Cost: $600-1,500
- Features: Glass door (see stock), stainless interior

**Portable Cool Boxes** (Budget Option)
- Engel, Waeco, Dometic: 40-60L
- Cost: $400-800
- Pros: Portable, 12V or 240V power
- Cons: Smaller capacity (limits all-day events)

**Milk Storage Tips**
- Buy fresh daily (mobile cart, no overnight storage)
- 2-hour rule: Discard milk left out >2 hours (food safety)
- Alt milks: Oat, almond, soy (stock based on demand)

### Generator (Outdoor Events) (350 words)

**When You Need a Generator**
- Outdoor events (no building power)
- Parks, beaches, festivals, construction sites
- Backup power (in case venue power fails)

**Generator Specifications**

*Power Output*
- Minimum: 2000W continuous (espresso machine + grinder)
- Recommended: 2500-3000W (headroom for fridge, lights)
- Surge capacity: 3000W+ (machine startup draw)

*Inverter vs Standard*
- Inverter: Quiet, clean power (protect electronics), expensive
- Standard: Loud, affordable, sufficient for most
- Recommendation: Inverter if budget allows (noise complaints)

*Fuel*
- Petrol: Most common, widely available
- Tank capacity: 8-12L (6-8 hours runtime at 50% load)

**Top Generators (Australia)**
- Honda EU20i: $1,600-2,000 (quiet inverter, gold standard)
- Yamaha EF2000iS: $1,400-1,800 (quiet, reliable)
- Gentrax 2.5kW: $600-900 (budget option, louder)

**Generator Considerations**
- Noise: <60dB for events (quiet conversation level)
- Weight: 20-30kg (portable but heavy)
- Placement: 5-10m from cart (reduce noise, fumes)
- Extension cord: Heavy-duty 15-amp, <20m

### Maintenance & Longevity (300 words)

**Daily Maintenance**
- Backflush machine (group head cleaning)
- Wipe down surfaces (cart, machine, grinder)
- Empty knock box, drip tray
- Purge grinder (fresh dose)

**Weekly Maintenance**
- Deep clean steam wand (milk buildup)
- Clean grinder burrs (remove oils)
- Descale machine (if hard water area)
- Sanitize cart surfaces

**Monthly Maintenance**
- Replace water filter (machine protection)
- Full machine cleaning (Cafetto soak)
- Inspect cart (wheels, tanks, seals)
- Calibrate grinder (check dose weight)

**Annual Servicing**
- Professional machine service: $200-400
- Replace seals, gaskets (wear items)
- Grinder burr replacement (every 500-800kg beans)
- Cart inspection (structural, tanks, electrical)
