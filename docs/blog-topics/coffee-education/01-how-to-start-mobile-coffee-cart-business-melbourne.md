### Legal Requirements & Registrations (600 words)

**Business Structure**
- **Sole trader**: Simplest, report under personal tax (ABN required)
- **Company**: Limited liability, more credibility, higher costs
- **Partnership**: Two+ co-owners, shared responsibility

**Register Your Business (Step-by-Step)**

**Step 1: Australian Business Number (ABN)**
- Apply: business.gov.au/ABN
- Free, instant approval (usually)
- Required for invoicing, tax, GST

**Step 2: Business Name (Optional)**
- Register: asic.gov.au
- Cost: $37 for 1 year, $87 for 3 years
- Only needed if trading under name other than your own

**Step 3: Goods and Services Tax (GST)**
- Register if turnover >$75,000/year
- Most coffee cart businesses exceed this (register early)
- Add 10% to prices, remit quarterly to ATO

**Step 4: Food Business Registration**
- Register with local council (e.g., City of Melbourne)
- Cost: $150-400/year (council-dependent)
- Required before operating
- Inspection may be required

**Step 5: Food Safety Supervisor Certificate**
- At least one staff member certified
- Training: SITXFSA005 or SITXFSA006 (online or in-person)
- Cost: $150-300
- Valid indefinitely (refresh every 5 years recommended)

**Permits & Licenses**

**Mobile Food Vendor Permit** (Event-Specific)
- Required for: Public spaces (parks, streets, beaches)
- NOT required for: Private venues (function centers, corporate offices, backyards)
- Application: Local council (2-4 weeks ahead)
- Cost: $50-300/event

**Roadside Trading Permit** (Ongoing Markets)
- Required for: Regular market stalls, fixed public locations
- Cost: $200-800/year (location-dependent)
- City of Melbourne most expensive (CBD locations)

**Insurance Requirements**

**Public Liability Insurance (Essential)**
- Coverage: $10-20 million
- Covers: Guest injury, property damage, legal defense
- Cost: $500-1500/year
- Shop around: Compare quotes from 3-5 insurers

**Product Liability Insurance**
- Usually bundled with public liability
- Covers: Food-borne illness claims

**Workers Compensation** (If Hiring Staff)
- Legally required in Victoria
- Cost: Based on payroll, industry risk rating

**Equipment Insurance** (Recommended)
- Covers: Theft, damage, fire
- Cost: $300-1000/year

### Startup Costs Breakdown (500 words)

**Essential Equipment**

**Coffee Cart**
- New: $8,000-25,000 (depends on features, size, branding)
- Used: $4,000-12,000 (check condition, equipment included)
- DIY build: $2,000-6,000 (if skilled, time-intensive)

**Espresso Machine**
- Entry-level 1-group: $3,000-6,000 (Rancilio, La Marzocco)
- Mid-range 2-group: $8,000-15,000 (higher capacity)
- Used: -30-50% (inspect carefully, service history)

**Grinder**
- Quality grinder essential: $800-2,500
- Brands: Mazzer, Eureka, Baratza Sette

**Water System**
- Onboard tanks (90L fresh, 36L waste): $500-1,200 (often included with cart)
- Water filtration: $200-500 (protects machine)

**Refrigeration**
- Milk fridge (undercounter): $600-1,500

**Generator** (If Outdoor Events)
- 2000W+ portable: $800-2,000
- Quiet inverter models preferred (less noise)

**Smallwares & Accessories**
- Milk jugs, tampers, cleaning supplies, knock box: $300-600
- Serving supplies (cups, lids, napkins): $200-400 (initial stock)

**Total Equipment Cost: $15,000-40,000**
- Budget setup: $15,000-20,000
- Mid-range: $25,000-35,000
- Premium: $40,000-60,000

**Ongoing Costs (Monthly)**
- Coffee beans: $300-800 (depends on volume)
- Milk & consumables: $200-500
- Cups, lids, napkins: $150-300
- Insurance: $100-200/month (paid annually)
- Vehicle costs (fuel, maintenance): $200-400
- Marketing: $50-200
- Licenses & permits: $50-150/month (amortized)
- **Total monthly: $1,050-2,550**

### Equipment Deep Dive (400 words)

**Choosing the Right Cart**

*Size Considerations*
- Small (1.2m x 0.7m): Solo vendor, easy transport, limited space
- Medium (1.5m x 0.8m): Most common, good balance
- Large (2m x 1m): High capacity, dual baristas, harder to transport

*Features to Look For*
- Stainless steel construction (durable, food-safe)
- Locking wheels (stability)
- Storage shelves (cups, beans, supplies)
- Professional finish (first impression matters)
- Weather protection (roof, side panels)

*New vs Used*
- New: Warranty, customize, financing available
- Used: Save $4,000-10,000, faster start, inspect thoroughly

**Espresso Machine Selection**

*Key Features*
- Temperature stability (PID control)
- Steam power (important for milk drinks)
- Reliability (brands: La Marzocco, Rancilio, Nuova Simonelli)
- Serviceability (local technician availability)

*1-Group vs 2-Group*
- 1-group: Sufficient for most events, lower cost
- 2-group: Faster service, handles 2 baristas, higher cost

**Don't Cheap Out On**
- Grinder (affects coffee quality dramatically)
- Water filtration (protects $8,000+ machine)
- Cart build quality (you'll move it hundreds of times)

**Can Skimp On (Initially)**
- Fancy cart aesthetics (upgrade later with vinyl wrap)
- Premium accessories (gold-plated tampers, etc.)
- Newest machine models (used machines work fine)

### Pricing Your Services (400 words)

**Pricing Models**

**Hourly Rate**
- Most common for events
- Melbourne range: $450-800 for 2 hours (1 barista)
- Includes: Setup, service, pack-down, supplies

**Per-Cup Pricing**
- Alternative for large uncertain events
- Range: $5-7/cup (vs retail $5.50-6.50)
- Minimum: 50-100 cups usually

**Package Pricing**
- Wedding Package: $850 (2 hours, 2 baristas, 150 cups)
- Corporate Package: $650 (2 hours, 1 barista, branded cups)
- Festival Day Rate: $1200 (8 hours, revenue share alternative)

**Factors Affecting Price**
- Event type (weddings premium, corporate mid, private budget)
- Day of week (Saturday +20%, Sunday +10%, weekday standard)
- Location (CBD +10-15%, regional +travel fee)
- Guest count (150+ may require 2 baristas = higher price)
- Season (Dec-Feb peak = +10-20%)

**Common Add-Ons**
- Second barista: +$150-200
- Extra hour: +$100-150
- Custom branding (cups, signage): +$50-150
- Generator (outdoor): +$100-150
- Travel beyond 30km: +$50-100

### Operations & Service Excellence (400 words)

**Event Day Workflow**

**Pre-Event (Week Before)**
- Confirm details: Time, location, guest count, menu
- Site visit (if unfamiliar venue)
- Shop for supplies (beans, milk, cups)

**Event Day (2-3 Hours Before Service)**
- Load vehicle (checklist: cart, machine, beans, milk, generator, etc.)
- Arrive 60-90 min before service
- Setup: Position cart, power, machine warm-up (20-30 min), test shots

**During Service**
- Friendly, professional demeanor
- Efficient workflow (minimize wait times)
- Engage guests (coffee education, recommendations)
- Monitor supplies (don't run out of milk!)

**Post-Service**
- Pack down (30-40 min)
- Clean up area (leave it pristine)
- Thank organizer (mention business cards left)

**Service Quality Standards**
- Consistent coffee quality (dial in grinder each event)
- 5-10 min wait times max (adjust workflow if longer)
- Clean, professional appearance (cart, barista, setup)
- Dietary accommodations (alt milks, decaf)

**Building Reputation**
- Deliver on promises (show up on time, deliver what's quoted)
- Go above and beyond (little touches: smile, compliment, conversation)
- Request reviews (post-event email: "Loved serving you! Review on TBR?")
- Capture content (photos, testimonials for marketing)

### Scaling Your Business (350 words)

**Year 1: Foundation**
- Goal: 50-100 events
- Focus: Quality, reputation, reviews
- Revenue: $20,000-40,000

**Year 2: Growth**
- Goal: 100-150 events
- Focus: Raise prices, premium clients
- Revenue: $50,000-80,000

**Year 3+: Scale**
- **Option 1**: Add second cart + barista (double capacity)
- **Option 2**: Corporate contracts (recurring revenue)
- **Option 3**: Hire staff (you manage, others operate)
- **Option 4**: Niche specialization (weddings only, premium pricing)
- Revenue: $80,000-150,000+

**Hiring Staff**
- When: Booked 3-4 events/week consistently
- Barista pay: $25-35/hour (experience-dependent)
- Training: Shadow you for 3-5 events before solo
- Workers comp required (insurance)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid (300 words)

1. **Skipping insurance** (one incident = bankruptcy)
2. **Cheap equipment** (breaks constantly, ruins reputation)
3. **Underpricing** (can't sustain business on $300 events)
4. **Poor grinder** (excellent machine + poor grinder = bad coffee)
5. **Overpromising capacity** (100 guests, 15-min service = disaster)
6. **No online presence** (invisible = no bookings)
7. **Not responding quickly** (inquiries ghost if you wait 24 hours)
8. **Saying yes to everything** (burnout, quality suffers)
9. **No contracts** (he-said-she-said disputes)
10. **Forgetting to enjoy it** (love coffee, love people, love events)
