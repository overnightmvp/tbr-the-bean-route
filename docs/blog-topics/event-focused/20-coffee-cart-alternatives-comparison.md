### Option 1: Coffee Cart (The Standard) (500 words)

**What It Is**
- Mobile espresso setup on wheeled cart
- Self-contained (water tanks, power via outlet/generator)
- Typical size: 1.5m x 0.8m footprint
- Setup time: 30-40 minutes
- 1 barista serves 60-80 cups/hour

**Best For**
- Weddings (aesthetic appeal)
- Corporate events (professional presentation)
- Medium-sized events (50-200 guests)
- Indoor or outdoor (versatile)
- Events where coffee is a feature (not just functional need)

**Advantages**
- Visual appeal (Instagram-worthy, conversation piece)
- Full espresso menu (lattes, cappuccinos, specialty drinks)
- Flexible placement (fits through doorways, elevators)
- Moderate capacity (good for most events)
- Moderate cost ($500-800 for 2-3 hours)

**Disadvantages**
- Limited capacity (one barista = 80 cups/hour max)
- Requires power (extension cord or generator)
- Weather-dependent (outdoor needs marquee)
- Setup time (30-40 min before service)

**Pricing**
- Standard: $550-700 for 2 hours
- Large events (2 baristas): $800-1200
- Weekend premium: +10-20%

**Case Study: 150-Guest Wedding**
- Setup: Garden ceremony, 1-hour cocktail hour
- Solution: Coffee cart with 2 baristas
- Result: 85 cups served, 5-10 min wait times, very popular
- Cost: $850

### Option 2: Coffee Truck (High Capacity) (500 words)

**What It Is**
- Full mobile café in truck/van
- Built-in power (generator), large water tanks (200L+)
- Multiple espresso machines possible
- Setup time: 15-20 minutes (faster than cart)
- 2-3 baristas serve 150-200+ cups/hour

**Best For**
- Large events (300+ guests)
- Festivals and markets (all-day service)
- Street activations (public spaces)
- High-volume corporate events
- Multi-day events (truck stays on-site)

**Advantages**
- High capacity (multiple baristas, multiple machines)
- Self-powered (generator = no venue power needed)
- Large water supply (no refills needed)
- Weather-protected (baristas inside truck)
- Branding opportunities (truck wrap = mobile billboard)
- Faster setup (less equipment to assemble)

**Disadvantages**
- Requires vehicle access (wide loading area, street parking)
- Higher cost (+$300-500 vs cart)
- Less aesthetic appeal (industrial vs charming cart)
- Cannot fit indoors (obviously)
- Noise (generator) concerns

**Pricing**
- Standard: $1200-1800 for 6-8 hours
- Multi-day discounts: -15-20%
- Festival day rate: $1500-2500 (revenue share alternative)

**Case Study: Melbourne Food Festival**
- Event: 3-day festival, 5000+ attendees daily
- Solution: Coffee truck with 3 baristas
- Result: 1800 cups/day average, consistent quality
- Cost: $4500 for 3 days ($1500/day)

### Option 3: Barista Station (Minimalist) (450 words)

**What It Is**
- Portable espresso machine setup on table/counter
- No wheeled cart (uses existing venue furniture)
- Requires: Power, water access, counter space
- Setup time: 20-30 minutes (faster than cart)
- 1 barista serves 60-80 cups/hour

**Best For**
- Indoor corporate events (breakrooms, conference halls)
- Budget-conscious events (slightly cheaper than cart)
- Venues with existing counter space (utilize what's there)
- Events where aesthetic less important (function > form)
- Multi-day conferences (permanent setup)

**Advantages**
- Lower cost (-$50-150 vs cart)
- Faster setup (less equipment)
- Flexible space (adapts to venue layout)
- Professional coffee quality (full espresso menu)
- Less intrusive (blends into venue)

**Disadvantages**
- No visual appeal (not a feature, just functional)
- Requires venue furniture (table, counter)
- Less mobile (can't easily relocate mid-event)
- Setup looks less polished (wires, equipment visible)

**Pricing**
- Standard: $450-650 for 2 hours (10-20% less than cart)

**Case Study: Tech Conference**
- Event: 3-day conference, 200 attendees
- Setup: Barista station in conference center breakroom
- Service: All-day (8am-5pm) across 3 days
- Solution: Permanent station, baristas in 4-hour shifts
- Cost: $3600 for 3 days ($400/shift × 3 shifts/day × 3 days)

### Option 4: Batch Brew Station (Speed & Volume) (400 words)

**What It Is**
- Large-format batch coffee brewers (not espresso)
- Pre-brewed coffee in thermal carafes
- Self-serve or staffed
- Setup time: 10-15 minutes
- Capacity: 100+ cups/hour (self-serve)

**Best For**
- Very large events (500+ guests, tight timeline)
- Construction sites (high volume, simple coffee)
- Budget events (lower cost per cup)
- Morning events (hot black coffee + milk station)
- Events where speed > coffee quality

**Advantages**
- Highest capacity (self-serve = no queue bottleneck)
- Lowest cost ($200-400 vs $550+ for espresso)
- Fastest service (guests serve themselves)
- Simplest setup (plug in, brew, done)

**Disadvantages**
- Lower quality (batch brew < espresso drinks)
- No specialty drinks (just black coffee, add milk/sugar)
- No "experience" factor (functional only)
- Coffee degrades over time (needs fresh batches every 30-45 min)
- Less impressive (guests may perceive as "cheap")

**Pricing**
- Equipment rental: $100-200
- Staffed service: $200-400 for 3-4 hours
- Coffee supplies: $50-100 (50-100 cups)
- Total: $350-700 (vs $550-800 for espresso cart)

**Case Study: Construction Site Safety Meeting**
- Event: Monthly safety meeting, 120 workers, 7am start
- Setup: Batch brew station, self-serve
- Coffee: Simple black coffee, milk, sugar
- Result: All 120 workers served in 15 minutes (vs 1.5 hours with espresso cart)
- Cost: $250 (equipment + staff + supplies)

### Option 5: DIY Coffee Station (Budget Option) (400 words)

**What It Is**
- Consumer-grade coffee maker (Nespresso, drip brewer)
- Self-service setup (guests make own coffee)
- Hosted by venue or event organizer (no vendor)
- Setup time: 5-10 minutes
- Capacity: Limited by machine (20-40 cups/hour)

**Best For**
- Very small events (<25 guests)
- Informal gatherings (family, friends)
- Ultra-budget events (no budget for vendor)
- Events with coffee as "nice to have" (not essential)

**Advantages**
- Lowest cost ($50-150 total)
- Maximum control (you manage it)
- No vendor coordination needed
- Flexible timing (available entire event)

**Disadvantages**
- Lowest quality (consumer equipment, self-made)
- Hosts must manage (make coffee, restock, clean up)
- Limited capacity (machine bottleneck)
- No professional presentation
- Potential mess (spills, grounds, etc.)

**When It Makes Sense**
- Backyard BBQ with 15 friends (casual)
- Morning work meeting (<20 people)
- Family celebration (grandma's 80th birthday)
- Budget under $300 (can't afford vendor)

**When to Avoid**
- Professional events (corporate, client-facing)
- Weddings (quality + presentation matter)
- Large events (>40 guests, insufficient capacity)
- When host wants to enjoy event (not make coffee)

**DIY Cost Breakdown**
- Coffee maker rental: $50-100 (or own machine)
- Coffee beans: $20-40 (quality beans for 50 cups)
- Milk, sugar, cups: $30-50
- Total: $100-190

### Decision Framework: Which Option to Choose? (500 words)

**Choose Coffee Cart If:**
- Guest count: 50-200
- Budget: $500-800
- Aesthetic matters (weddings, client-facing)
- Indoor or outdoor venue
- Want full espresso menu
- Coffee as event feature

**Choose Coffee Truck If:**
- Guest count: 300+
- Budget: $1200-2000+
- Outdoor only (no indoor access)
- Multi-day event
- High volume essential
- Public space activation

**Choose Barista Station If:**
- Guest count: 50-150
- Budget: $400-650
- Indoor corporate event
- Venue has counter space
- Aesthetic less important
- Function > form

**Choose Batch Brew If:**
- Guest count: 150-500+
- Budget: $300-500
- Speed essential (tight timeline)
- Simple coffee sufficient (no espresso needed)
- Construction sites, morning meetings

**Choose DIY If:**
- Guest count: <25
- Budget: <$300
- Informal event (family, friends)
- No professional presentation needed
- Host willing to manage coffee

**Questions to Ask Yourself:**
1. How many guests?
2. What's the budget?
3. How important is aesthetic/presentation?
4. Indoor or outdoor venue?
5. What's the event type (formal vs casual)?
6. Do guests expect professional coffee or just caffeine?
7. How much time for coffee service?

### Hybrid Solutions (300 words)

**Coffee Cart + Batch Brew**
- Cart for espresso drinks (50% of guests)
- Batch brew for simple black coffee (50% of guests)
- Splits queue, speeds up service
- Cost: $700 (cart) + $200 (batch) = $900 total

**Multiple Carts (Different Offerings)**
- Cart 1: Hot espresso drinks
- Cart 2: Iced/cold brew drinks
- Splits queue, offers variety
- Best for: Large summer events

**Cart + DIY Tea Station**
- Professional coffee cart
- Self-serve tea station (host provides)
- Accommodates non-coffee drinkers
- Cost: $600 (cart) + $50 (DIY tea) = $650
